About Acorn Software Products, Inc.

Starting in 1979 Acorn Software Products was one of the leading software publishers in the United States. The catalog included games, productivity tools, educational products, and utilities for the Radio Shack TRS-80 and Atari computers.

From 1979 to 1982 Acorn published over 30 titles in a wide variety of categories, advertised monthly with display ads monthly in every issue of 80-Microcomputing and in other magazines, plus launched the careers of a number of authors. The authors were always promiently featured in packaging and advertising.

The company was founded in 1979 by Daphne Schor, Ira Hamburg, Tom Stibolt, and Ray Daly. In July 1983 Banbury Books purchased Acorn.

Notable Achievements

  • Daphne Schor was the President and one of the first women to lead a major software publisher.
  • By publishing "Formula 1" in 1982, Acorn was Sid Mieir's first publisher. He later gained fame at MicroProse and Firaxis where he authored many more titles including Pirates, Railroad Tycoon, and Civilization.
  • Acorn was recognized for the packaging design which worked for both retail and mail order. The layout of this site mimics that design.
