Acorn Software Products, Inc.

For over three years Acorn Software Products was one of the leading publishers in the United States. The catalog included categories of games, productivity tools, educational products, and utilities for the Radio Shack TRS-80 and Atari computers.

From 1979 until 1982 Acorn published over 30 titles in a wide variety of categories, advertised with display ads monthly in every issue of 80-Microcomputing and other magazines, and launched the careers of a number of authors. The authors were always promiently featured in packaging and advertising.

The company was founded in 1979 by Daphne Schor, Ira Hamburg, Tom Stibolt, and Ray Daly. In July 1983 Banbury Books purchased Acorn.

Notable Achievements

  • Daphne Schor was the President and one of the first women to lead a major software publisher.
  • By publishing "Formula 1 Racing" in 1982, Acorn was Sid Mieir's first publisher. He went on to gain aclaim at MicroProse and Firaxis where he published many more titles including Pirates, Railroad Tycoon, and Civilization.
  • Acorn started advertising in the premier issue 80-Microcomputing magazine until it became the third largest in the United States 30 issues later.
